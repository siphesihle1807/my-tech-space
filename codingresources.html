Where to Start?

There are numerous resources available to help you gain the tech skills you need(or want), whether you're just starting or looking to advance your knowledge, believe me when I say, you will want a piece of every pie. Below is a list of some of the top resources, academies, and learnerships you can explore:

Online Platforms

  • freeCodeCamp: A free platform offering interactive coding lessons and certifications in web development, data science, and more. Features projects and challenges to build a portfolio.
  • Codecademy: Offers both free and paid courses on various programming languages and technologies, including Python, JavaScript, and HTML/CSS. Interactive exercises enhance learning.
  • Coursera: Features a wide range of tech courses from top universities and companies. Audit many courses for free or pay for certification and degrees.
  • Pluralsight: Provides extensive courses on software development, IT operations, data, and more. Includes learning paths, assessments, and hands-on labs.
  • Khan Academy: Offers free lessons on computer science, programming, and mathematics with interactive exercises and instructional videos.
  • SoloLearn: A mobile-first platform for free coding courses in various languages, including interactive exercises and community forums.
  • Udemy: Provides a wide range of courses on programming and web development with options for free and paid content. Frequent discounts available.
  • Mimo: Offers bite-sized coding lessons and projects designed for learning on the go, ideal for busy schedules.

Learnerships and Bootcamps

  • WeThinkCode: Offers free, full-time coding education through a rigorous selection process and project-based learning, focusing on problem-solving skills.
  • Umuzi: Provides tech learnerships and internships with a focus on upskilling and employment for young talent in various technology fields.
  • ALX: Delivers transformative learning programs through project-based training, aiming to build a skilled workforce in tech and other fields.
  • Le Wagon: Provides immersive coding bootcamps focusing on web development and data science with hands-on projects for practical experience.
